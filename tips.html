Travel Tips


Tips for Beginners

  • Research your destination(such as the culture and social norms). Traveling can be overwhelming, it is important to know what to expect before you go
  • When planning your itinerary, leave some flexibility within your schedule that will account for if you end up getting lost so you don't feel stress
  • If you are planning to travel internationally, buy your SIM cards/portable Wi-Fi devices at home. You will thank yourself later when you get to offyour plane and have access to the internet right away
  • Make sure you call your banks and inform them of your travel plans. This will prevent your cards from being blocked while you are abroad

Tips that People Overlook

  • In case of itinerary changes, always have a backup plan. Unexpected weather changes will affect your plans and when this happens, you will be glad you have a backup plan
  • If applicable, learn a few basic language phrases of the country you are visiting. It is important to remember that not everyone speaks English and it is respectful to learn enough to get by.
  • Invest in quality travel insurance. Last thing you want to be hit with is a medical bill after spending X amount of money on your trip. Travel insurance is a must for international travel

Packing Tips

  • Comfortable shoes are a must for any travel adventure. A lot of people will aim for aesthetics but it is important to take into consideration the amount of walking you will be doing.
  • If traveling by plane, pack any liquids in your carry-on luggage. Liquids are more than likely to be confiscated if they are in your checked luggage. Make sure to check the TSA website for the most up to date information on what is allowed and what is not allowed in your carry-on luggage
  • Packing cubes can greatly help organize your belongings and maximize space in your luggage.
  • Optional Tip: Less is more. Depending one where you decide to travel to, it might be better to pack lighter and buy items locally. This will give you a more authentic experience and you'll have more souvenirs
